# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Film lovers, critics, creators, students, and industry professionals using 35mm to follow people, discuss cinema, log and review films, curate lists, and build a public film identity. The native app serves frequent, touch-first social and catalog workflows where clarity and speed matter more than decorative novelty.

## Product Purpose

35mm combines a social film feed with profiles, film logs and reviews, comments, follows, notifications, discovery, lists, and watchlists. The iOS app should make publishing and exploring film culture feel immediate while preserving canonical 35mm identities and the production behavior of the shared REST platform.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic, editorial, conversational. The experience should feel informed and intentional without becoming precious, dense, or theatrical. Warm red provides restrained emphasis; content and people remain the focus.

## Anti-references

- A generic social-media clone with interchangeable blue accents and engagement-first visual noise.
- An overdecorated cinema theme built from heavy gradients, glass surfaces, film-strip ornament, or novelty motion.
- A cramped utility interface with undersized labels, uneven alignment, ambiguous icons, or floating menus detached from their trigger and screen context.
- A marketing-style editorial page that sacrifices familiar native controls or efficient scanning for display typography.

## Design Principles

- Put film identity and human voice ahead of chrome.
- Preserve familiar iOS interaction patterns and consistent component vocabulary.
- Use hierarchy, spacing, and restrained accent color to clarify state instead of decorating it.
- Keep social reads bounded and responsive; progressive disclosure should protect focus and scale.
- Match the mobile-web information architecture while adapting presentation to native iOS conventions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG AA contrast intent and Apple accessibility conventions. Support Dynamic Type, VoiceOver, 44-point minimum touch targets, semantic color adaptation, Reduce Motion, and non-color indicators for selection and state. Long names, localized copy, missing media, and narrow or multitasking layouts must remain usable.
